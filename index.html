Part 2: Learning to tell stories in different forms/ mediums

What do you see/notice?

As someone who is terrified of insects but entranced by their colors and textures, I wanted to capture them in a surreal piece. This irrational fear makes it difficult for me to see insects as living creatures and easy for me to justify killing them. Therefore, I wanted to examine how similar bugs and humans are through the ways in which humans can be dehumanized and bugs be humanized. I did this through two main methods—the size and color of the bug, and the metallic texture of the human. The beetle is a vibrant green, a color often connected to vitality. It is also an obnoxious size for a bug, allowing it to be compared to the human at the same scale. The human has a metallic texture on its cap—giving a techno futuristic, almost robotic, appearance to the viewer. This separates the human from its human qualities. There is also another layer of nuance to the cap on its head—it resembles the beetle’s exoskeleton. This further highlights the similarities between insects and humans.
